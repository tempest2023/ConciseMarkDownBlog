# About Me

I'm Tao Ren, a Senior Full-stack Software Engineer, startup founder, and researcher focused on AI agents, full-stack product systems, and structure-aware machine intelligence.

I joined [Newfront](https://www.newfront.com/) in October 2025 after leaving TikTok, and I currently work on internal insurance AI systems as a Senior Full-stack Software Engineer. My work centers on the Placement AI Agent initiative, agent-assisted workflows, high-trust product UX, and production software for complex broker operations. On January 27, 2026, [WTW completed its acquisition of Newfront](https://investors.wtwco.com/news-releases/news-release-details/wtw-completes-acquisition-newfront), bringing Newfront's technology-enabled broking model and AI platform into WTW's broader strategy.

In summer 2025, I co-founded [Tira AI](https://tira.tempest.fun) with Sam Liu, a UC Berkeley alumnus. We explored AI-native execution products, received top-10% feedback in YC S25, and developed traction through conversations with founder programs and early-stage investors including PearX, Berkeley SkyDeck, Techstars, Antler, 500 Global, Beta University, and others, with a subset of those conversations progressing to preliminary investment interest.

I also enjoy building fast, ambitious products around emerging interfaces. Most recently, I built [AgentShelf](https://github.com/tempest2023/AgentShelf), a GEO Readiness Console that uses Generative UI to make agent-user interaction visible, inspectable, and operational for commerce teams.

On the research side, I co-authored [TreeDiff: AST-Guided Code Generation with Diffusion LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.01473), which was accepted to SURGeLLM at ACL 2026, and I currently have a systems paper under review at ACM Transactions on Embedded Computing Systems: `RED: Adaptive Real-Time DAG Scheduling for Robotic Inference under Environmental Dynamics`.

Previously, I worked across TikTok, Ant Group, Ansys, and the University of Pittsburgh. That mix of startup execution, large-scale product engineering, and applied research is what shapes how I build today.
